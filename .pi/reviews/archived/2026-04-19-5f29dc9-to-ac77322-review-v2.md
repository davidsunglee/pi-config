#### Important (Should Fix)

**1. Final-wave debug scope inconsistency — Step 11 `(a)` is unreachable for deferred regressions.**
- File: `agent/skills/execute-plan/SKILL.md`, Step 11 Pass/fail classification + final-wave `(a)` menu
- Issue: Pass/fail classification says **Pass** when `new_regressions_after_deferment` is empty. If `deferred_integration_regressions ≠ ∅` but `new_regressions_after_deferment == ∅`, the wave passes Step 11 and advances to Step 15. The final-wave `(a)` text ("include those identifiers in the debugging scope as well; final-wave debugging must clear both sets") is therefore unreachable — the menu only fires on a Step 11 fail.
- Fix: Either (a) make final-wave pass/fail depend on `new_regressions_after_deferment ∪ deferred_integration_regressions`, or (b) drop the "include those identifiers in the debugging scope as well" sentence from Step 11's final-wave `(a)` and instead add a cross-reference to Step 15's gate for deferred-regression handling. Option (b) is less disruptive.

**2. Sub-task retry budget accounting is undefined.**
- File: `agent/skills/execute-plan/SKILL.md`, Step 9.5 §5 interacting with Step 12 shared counter
- Issue: When a task is split into sub-tasks, it's unclear whether the split consumes retries from the parent's budget, and whether sub-tasks inherit the parent's exhausted counter or get fresh 3-retry budgets. In the worst case, split-into-sub-tasks bypasses retry hardening (parent exhausted → split → 9 more effective retries).
- Fix: State explicitly in Step 9.5 §5 (or Step 12's shared-counter note): "The split itself counts as 1 retry against the parent's budget; each sub-task inherits the parent's remaining retry count."

**3. Step 9.7 §4 exhausted-budget wording is circular.**
- File: `agent/skills/execute-plan/SKILL.md`, Step 9.7 §4
- Issue: "an exhausted budget on a `(r)` choice is surfaced to the user as `(r)` (offer a different model/context) or `(x)` — never a silent skip" reads as an infinite loop. `(r)` is the option the user just picked.
- Fix: Replace with explicit next step, e.g., "… the user is re-prompted with the Step 9.7 §3 correctness/scope menu, where `(r)` now requires specifying a different model or additional context; the automatic retry counter does not advance further, but each manual re-dispatch still costs a retry."

**4. Step 9.5 §5 pre-commit invariant stated only in Step 10.2 — not at the split site.**
- File: `agent/skills/execute-plan/SKILL.md`, Step 9.5 §5 vs Step 10.2
- Issue: The constraint "sub-task dispatches MUST occur pre-commit" lives in Step 10.2's `{DIFF_CONTEXT}` bullet. The logical home is Step 9.5 §5 (where the split happens). Someone reading Step 9.5 has no cue this invariant exists.
- Fix: Add one normative line in Step 9.5 §5: "Sub-task dispatches run pre-commit — their changes must remain uncommitted at the point Step 10 dispatches the verifier; see Step 10.2 for the fallback if this is violated."

**5. Step 15 deferred-regression gate recomputes reconciliation but gates only on `still_failing_deferred`.**
- File: `agent/skills/execute-plan/SKILL.md`, Step 15 gate protocol step 2
- Issue: The gate runs the Step 11 reconciliation rule, which computes both `still_failing_deferred` and `new_regressions_after_deferment`. But Step 15 only gates on `still_failing_deferred`. Brand-new regressions appearing on the Step 15 test run are silently ignored.
- Fix: Either (a) add an explicit "and assert `new_regressions_after_deferment` is empty" check to Step 15's gate-on clause, or (b) document explicitly that Step 15's scope is intentionally limited to deferred regressions and Step 11 is the last gate for brand-new regressions.
