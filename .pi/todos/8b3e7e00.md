## Context
Reviewed `agent/skills/execute-plan/SKILL.md` on `HEAD` against `origin/main`.

## Independent appraisal
The `HEAD` version is substantially more complex, but the complexity is broadly justified by materially better verification and completion semantics.

### Why the added complexity is worth it
Keep the stronger architecture introduced on `HEAD`:
- Separate verifier-based Step 10 instead of orchestrator self-verification
- Mandatory `Verify:` recipes on acceptance criteria
- Typed `DONE_WITH_CONCERNS` handling with a dedicated wave-level concerns gate
- Identifier-based integration regression tracking (`baseline_failures`, `deferred_integration_regressions`, `new_regressions_after_deferment`)
- Final integration regression gate in Step 15
- No-skip semantics for unresolved failed/blocked tasks

## Main concern
The current `HEAD` skill is too legalistic / over-specified in places. The model is better, but the prose could be trimmed significantly without losing rigor.

## Keep / trim / drop guidance

### Keep
- Verifier architecture in Step 10
- `Verify:` recipe requirement
- Typed concerns + Step 9.7 gate
- Three-set integration regression model
- Final Step 15 regression gate
- No-skip rule for unresolved failures
- Shared retry-budget concept

### Trim
- Exact output-shape legalism and repeated malformed-output taxonomy
- Exact truncation-marker requirements and some evidence-format specifics
- Repeated retry-budget explanations
- Repeated blocked-gate / control-flow restatements
- Over-detailed concern-routing mechanics
- Over-explained distinction between Step 11 debugger flow and Step 15 final-gate debugger flow

### Drop or merge
- Exact literal marker-string requirements where semantic equivalence is fine
- Some protocol-correction micro-rules (e.g. elaborate untyped-concern recovery)
- Overly detailed pre-commit subtask diff carve-out; merge into a simpler verifier-diff rule
- Duplicate "never silently X" restatements

## Recommended next step
Refactor the skill to keep the `HEAD` verification architecture while reducing procedural repetition. Target roughly a 20-30% reduction in prose by:
1. Collapsing Steps 9.5 + 9.7 + 12 into a cleaner wave-gate model
2. Rewriting Step 10 as a concise verification protocol
3. Defining the integration-regression model once, then reusing it in Step 11 and Step 15
