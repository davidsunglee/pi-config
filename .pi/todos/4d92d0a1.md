## Summary
Start from the current (v2) `agent/skills/execute-plan/SKILL.md` and cut Step 9.7 (wave-level concerns checkpoint). Keep every other v2 addition. Revisit the deferred-integration-regression feature only if v2-minus-9.7 still feels unwieldy in practice.

## Context
Compared v2 (commit `14a89882`) against v1 (commit `5f29dc9e`). v2 grew from ~700 to ~1000 lines. The question: is the stronger verification worth the added complexity, and which direction is easier to reach a good middle ground from?

Ranking of v2 additions by ROI:

| v2 addition | Value | Complexity | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| Verifier subagent (Step 10) with per-criterion PASS/FAIL | high | moderate | keep |
| Identifier-based test tracking (Step 7) | high | low | keep |
| Step 15 final integration regression gate | high | low | keep |
| Shared retry counter + no-skip rule (Step 12) | moderate | low | keep |
| Typed concerns checkpoint (Step 9.7) | low | high | **cut** |
| Deferred integration regressions (Step 11 `(b)`, reconciliation, 3-set math) | moderate | high | borderline — leave for now |

Starting from v2 and cutting is safer than starting from v1 and re-adding, because the hard-to-get-right parts (verifier protocol, identifier extraction, three-set math, truncation rules) are already specified and presumably debugged in v2 — rewriting them from v1 risks reintroducing subtle bugs.

## Scope

Surgical removal of Step 9.7 only:

1. Delete Step 9.7 entirely (~100 lines).
2. Restore v1's Step 9 `DONE_WITH_CONCERNS` bullet: *"correctness/scope concerns must be addressed before verification; observations can be noted and execution continues"* — no typed-routing protocol, no combined concerns view.
3. Clean up cross-references that chain through 9.7:
   - Step 9.5 §2 and §6 (remove "→ Step 9.7" language; route directly from 9.5 exit to Step 10).
   - Step 10 precondition (drop the 9.7 reference).
   - Step 11 precondition (drop correctness/scope concern check inherited from 9.7).
   - Step 12 pacing footnote (drop the 9.7 mention).

## Out of scope (for now)

The deferred integration regressions feature. It is the next most questionable addition — dropping it would collapse the three-set model to two sets (`baseline_failures` vs `current_failing`), simplify Step 11's menus, and trim Step 15. But cutting it is ~4× the edit surface of cutting 9.7 and touches load-bearing cross-references in Steps 11, 12, 13, and 15. Defer unless v2-minus-9.7 keeps causing problems.
